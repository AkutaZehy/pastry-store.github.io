1.Reversed world, a little euphoria in the Post-Apocalyptic World

*Post-Apocalyptic is also henceforth referred to as P-A.

Frankly speaking, you have to watch Girls' Last Tour, thoroughly.
You see, many say that it's a quite depressive work and has an awfully heartbreaking ending. But that's perhaps interpret out of context since quite a few haven't read the whole story.
The girls are living in a post-apocalyptic world, after a war almost/actually wiped out the human race. So it's normal, exactly speaking, is environmental normal.
——Coolink_95

As mentioned, it's essential to base the understanding of the whole work in a P-A world, or misunderstanding occurs from time to time.
What does the work want to convey? Take your minutes to carefully think about that.
When you are ready, roll your eyesight down to receive a possible explanation.

i.

Let's describe the work first.
Desperation? No from one aspect. On the contrary maybe a little euphoria.
By the way, do you remember, when Kanazawa lost his maps, he cried out bluely, but when seperated, he smiled, staying opmistic and went on. When Ishii's aircraft crashed, she fell with a complex smile, consisting of hopelessness and frustration. We know their sad ending. Every nothing fought for their fate.

絶望と、仲良く。

That's words of Yuuri, also the spirit of the work.

ii.

In my opinion, the work reflects on our life, our growing up.
At the beginning Chito could't abandon his diary, Yuuri couldn't lose her gun. But when they climbed up,they gradually abandoned everything:when the Kettenkrad could no longer use, they took a nice bath, and went on by walking; Yuuri threw her gun away to make the journey easier; Chito burned her diary for warmth. And finally they reached the end, and finally had a long sleep.
So do us. We should learn to have somthing when we're young, but when we grow up, we gradually realize what is important while what is not. Having seperated from what we love, so that we can forth with less burden.
Also, the older we are, the less needs do we have. According to Maslow's hierarchy of needs, our needs are dived into 5 levels:Physiological needs, Safety needs, Love and belonging, Esteem and Self-actualization(Self-Transcendence needs). Roughly speaking, this work in P-A world actually shows a retrogression of needs. The girls cared more about their physiological needs than spiritual ones as the story advance. Somehow that's because they do had more physiological needs, but in other aspects, it shows a unique understanding of life:

When satisfied with everything, so we can face death without hesitation and regret.

iii.

As for Kanazawa and Ishii, maybe they just serve as a foil to main figures, showing other attitudes towards life.
In this work, we see many desperate moments, and we also should get along with those. To face the rough life with a flattened attitude, to enjoy the pure way of what ancient people do may be one explaionation of this work.

Generally speaking, I would conclude this opinion like this:

When Bible told us,
For to everyone who has will be given, and he will have more: but from him who has not, even what he has will be taken away.
I would like to say,
Be appreciated for what you have more, be peaceful for what you don't.
Get along well with desperation.

Morever.

Well, I have to say, this used to be the part I've forgotten for such a long time and caused me had a really narrow understanding of this work.
A little challenging this part, be patient and read the content below.
It's about humanity, nature and cycle.

To be honest, I haven't read many books about philosophy such as Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung (by Arthur Schopenhauer,1819), so my point of view is actually one-sided. However, the process of sharing is quite enjoyful.
Let's start with a simple question:
How will you define HUMAN or HUMANITY, in order to set them apart from other creatures?

The answer from me, is

WHAT'S DIFFERENT.

Well it may seem a bit obscure, then let me explain it to you.
Have an example first, just use Girl's Last Tour.
Chito, Yuuri, Kanazawa, Ishii and gramdpa, woman in the photo, people recorded, are traditional HUMANs, while autonomous machines, artificial intelligence managing the sixth tower, Nuko and Eryngiis are just HUMANOID.
And what's different?
My answer is:

MERCY.

You may be surprised at it, but maybe that's right:which is determined by OUR sociality and ideology.
Maybe your answer is life? Animals and plants are all alive.
Maybe your answer is language? Eryngiis had developed their language, superior to Chito and Yuuri's.
Maybe your answer is dedication? It's just social behaviour, community animals know this.
Maybe your answer is spirit? In my opinion, when the AI committed her suicide, she had finished her task to reach her spirit.

Why Mercy ?

We all understand the pain of creatures well when we are mature,
While repeating doing wrong things again and again.
When we finally decided to do something,
Then comes what we call Mercy.

We learn edifying after we learn to "Mercy".
Sometimes we surprisingly find that an animal learn to be something like so called "Human", however, they never learn to "Mercy", or at least we never lowered ourselves to call it "Mercy"

Without mercy, we stood, we fought, we invaded, we expanded our home from caves, to lands, to seas, to inside the earth, to beyond the sky, maybe further, unlimited?
With mercy, we started communicating, taming, saving lives from "man-made thunders", fighting with pens and ink.
Then we see the cycle, the nature, the thing we used to call them 'infinite'.

Take a minute for what you have just learnt.

4.Literary significance, universality and peculiarity

This work is connected to many famous works, but the ones I want to mention the most,are Waiting for Godot and Truman Show, based on which I made several changes in my video to make my point of view clear and obvious.

i. Waiting for Godot, what are you waiting for?

I strongly suggest you to read/watch the whole drama first, having enough understanding of it, or the content below will be hard for you to understand.

Ready? Let's answer a question first.
Who is Godot in the play?

When you find the answer for the question, you are close to my opinion.
My answer is:

There's no Godot, but he is to be waited.

Strange huh? Let's turn to another dimension.
If I ask, were the Girls getting upward, or downward? Were they getting further from home, or getting closer?
Think twice before you make sure, then you would get the point interesting here.
Think about the keys from their grandpa.
A contradictory but reasonable conclusion: they were departing from their home, but setting out for their home, right?

Waiting for Godot, or to be accurate in the story, going upward, is meaningless in a sense.
So do our daily lives. Give it a shot to seek for the origin to do one daily task, gradually more and more until everything you do everyday. You'll find it true.

You may get more and more confused when your understanding of this opinion getting deeper and deeper.
You may think, when life is a certain, why should myself struggling to make a life? Everything I do is in vain, why should I live?
You may be trapped in those strange thoughts, or you may never think about it. That doesn't matter.

One of my best friends gave me a good answer to explain this:

So if it's meaningless to live, so is death.
They are just a sign, a boolean flag, meaningless.
However, why do I choose to live? I'm just too used to this kind of life so I would trigger the SWITCH to change.

Go and figure out your own anwer, always remember:

We are waiting for Godot, but he said he is to come tomorrow.
